What Is NYSAA?

The NYSAA:Is part of the New York State testing program that measures the attainment of the State’s learning standards in areas of English language arts (ELA), mathematics, and science students with the most severe cognitive disabilities. Students assessed annually in ELA and mathematics in grades 3-8 and one at the secondary level prior to exiting school. In science, students are assessed in grades 4 and 8 and one time at the secondary level prior to exiting school. NYSAA eligible students are assessed using the Dynamic Learning Maps (DLM) alternate assessment system. The DLM alternate assessment system is a computer-delivered adaptive assessment that measures student achievement of the ELA, mathematics, and science standards at a to help inform instruction.

How does it work?

The New York: State Alternate Assessment (NYSAA) is a part of the New York State Testing Program. Starting with the 2013-14 administration, the NYSAA is based on a new test design with requirements that reflect the updated assessment. The NYSAA is a datafolio-style assessment in which students with severe cognitive disabilities demonstrate their performance toward achieving the New York State P-12 Common Core Learning Standards in English language arts and mathematics.
